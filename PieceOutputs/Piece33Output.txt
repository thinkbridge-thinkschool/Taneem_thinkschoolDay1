Day 5 — Verify in App Insights with your first KQL
====================================================

## KQL Query

requests
| where timestamp > ago(30m)
| summarize count(), p50=percentile(duration, 50), p99=percentile(duration, 99) by name
| order by p99 desc

## KQL Result

name                   | count | p50    | p99
-----------------------|-------|--------|--------
GET /api/quotes/       | 2     | 213ms  | 680ms
POST api/auth/login    | 2     | 465ms  | 616ms
GET /health            | 2     | 74ms   | 98ms

## Observation

GET /api/quotes/ surprised me the most — its p99 (680ms) was higher than
POST /api/auth/login (616ms). Login does bcrypt password hashing which is
intentionally slow, so I expected it to be the slowest. But /api/quotes
beat it at p99 because the first request hit the N+1 bug — 11 separate DB
queries instead of 1 — pushing the worst-case response time above even
bcrypt. After fixing the N+1, the second request dropped significantly,
which is why p50 (213ms) and p99 (680ms) are so far apart for that endpoint.
